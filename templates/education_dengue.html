What is Dengue?

Overview

Dengue is a viral infection transmitted by Aedes mosquitoes. Infections range from mild illness to severe dengue with plasma leakage and bleeding. Source: WHO fact sheet, CDC overview.

Common symptoms

  • High fever, headache, and pain behind the eyes. Source: CDC symptoms.
  • Muscle and joint pain, nausea or vomiting, rash, and fatigue. Source: CDC symptoms.

Warning signs

Seek urgent care if these appear after fever subsides. Source: WHO, CDC.

  • Severe abdominal pain or persistent vomiting
  • Bleeding from gums or nose, blood in vomit or stool
  • Lethargy, restlessness, or rapid breathing

How transmission happens

Infected mosquitoes transmit dengue when they bite humans. Human to mosquito to human is the typical cycle. Source: WHO.

Prevention

  • Eliminate mosquito breeding sites like standing water. Source: CDC prevention.
  • Use EPA-registered repellents and wear long sleeves and pants. Source: CDC prevention.
  • Use screens and nets to reduce bites. Source: WHO.
